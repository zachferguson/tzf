My name is Zach, and I build things.

Visit my github

I've been programming for over 4 years, because I love pawing at the keyboard for a few hours/days/weeks, and seeing something that existed in my mind become reality.

I started leaning to program with Python for fun with Raspberry Pi, and later added FreeCodeCamp, web development, and JavaScript. I'm passionate about programming, and started the Wilkes-Barre Programming Community Meetup group in 2016 to meet other programmers, create a community of like-minded people, and to give people interested in learning programming a place to go for support.

My main interests are back end and full stack web development, Python for anything and everything Python can be used for, and physical computing (robotics, automation, and IoT).

The technologies, languages, and software I use most often are Python, JavaScript, HTML and CSS, and Photoshop. I use Atom for web development, and Atom and PyCharm for python. I also use openSCAD to design parts for my 3D printer.

In the time I'm not working or programming, I'm active with jogging, exercise, hiking, kayaking, or at weekly trivia night. I cohabitate with two rescue cats.

I think two of the best feelings in the world are when you finally solve that problem, and when you finally get all the pieces put together and everything works.

Although I love making things, period, one thing I'm particularly passionate about is Pawtomatics.com; where I am slowly building up open source plans for pet and animal care related automated and IoT devices. The most important of these is the Cat Trap, which allows people participating in "Catch, Fix, and Release" feral cat population control programs to catch the right cat by remote trigger through the website.
